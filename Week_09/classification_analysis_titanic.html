Task 1b: When increasing the test size from 20% to 50%, the overall model accuracy improves from 0.78 to 0.81.

With a larger test set, performance estimates become more stable because the model is evaluated on a broader sample of passengers.

Class 0 (not survived): Recall increases from 0.83 0.87, meaning the model becomes better at correctly identifying non-survivors.

Class 1 (survived): Recall slightly decreases from 0.71 0.72, showing similar behavior but still weaker performance than for non-survivors.

Overall, the larger test set results in more reliable evaluation metrics and a slightly better accuracy, likely because the model generalizes well and benefits from being tested on more diverse data.

After removing Age and Sex_male, the feature importance changes strongly. Before removal, Age and Sex_male were the two most important predictors of survival. Once they are removed, the model shifts almost all importance to Fare, with Pclass becoming only weakly relevant.

Task 1e

When the variables Age and Sex_male are included, the Random Forest achieves an AUC of 0.86 (very strong discrimination ability).

After removing these two variables, the AUC drops to 0.70, indicating a clearly weaker model.

Conclusion: Age and Sex_male are essential predictors for survival. Removing them removes the strongest signal in the data, so the model becomes significantly less accurate and loses discriminative power.
